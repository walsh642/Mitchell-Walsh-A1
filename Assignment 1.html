Project Idea

Overview My project is called Deck Drone. It is built for the construction industry and is a robotic drone designed to mark out decks on construction sites. Marking out decks involves measuring off gridlines to precisely spot mark a section on the deck in which a penetration will be present after the concrete is poured. It is almost always a 2-person job and involves working in outdoor conditions. What if we could reduce the cost of labour and enhance the safety of workers, Deck Drone does exactly that.

Motivation

Performing deck work in construction is labour intensive. Workers are regularly bending over, lifting heavy equipment, working in direct sunlight and cold conditions. Statistics show that 37% of injury claims in construction are related to body stressing (Safe Work AUS). By implementing Deck Drone we can reduce the number of workers required to undertake the task of marking decks, and can further increase the safety and wellbeing of the worker. I have worked the construction industry for 16 years and I know the impact a robotic drone can have on reducing labour cost, and workers safety.

Description

Before we can see the benefits of Deck Drone, lets first understand the process of marking out decks. I am specifically going to explain the process of how a plumbing trade would perform this as I myself am a qualified plumber with many years’ experience in construction. The deck is an area of the site that has been formed with timbers or metal decking in preparation for a concrete pour. As we know, building aren’t just slabs of concrete, they have bathrooms, kitchens, laundries, and other areas which may require basins, toilets, sinks, floor waste etc. All these require to be installed with pipes for drainage of waste. This is where fire collars are placed. A fire collar allows for pipe to be cast into the slab whilst protecting the upper and lower-level floors if a fire was to occur. The image below is of a deck made from metal, and you can see the blue and green style fire collars in place.

ll these collars need to be placed in a specific location to where they are required eg bathroom area. As each collar has a specific location, the worker must read the provided plans and measure off gridlines which are marked out around the outer perimeter of the deck to acquire the location of the collars. This needs to be done for every collar, and some decks may have over 100 collars to mark out and place into position. Deck Drone can automate this entire process and can be performed with only 1 worker. Deck Drone works by placing a marker at each corner of the deck. For simplicity we will say our deck is a rectangle, so we have 1 marker at each corner. These markers are signals which identify the location of each other thus creating a perimeter. This information is uploaded to the drone, and it now has an area in which it can operate. The building plans displaying the location of each collar is uploaded to Deck Drone. The software then syncs the building plans to the perimeter captured using the signal markers. Now the worker is ready to run Deck Drone. The worker who can now simply sit in the shade with a laptop begins to operate Deck Drone. Each collar’s location is placed into a list on the software, and the worker simply attaches the collar to the drone. The worker then presses enter on the software and the drone delivers the collar to its exact location. The drone places the collar in position and staples it down into place, then returning to base to be reloaded with another collar. This process repeats until all collars are installed.

Tools and Technologies

This project requires tools and technologies which can produce the desired outcome of perimeter scanning and drone delivery. I believe this project requires a drone capable of being programmed to identify and operate within a set perimeter. It also needs to be durable enough to with stand outdoor conditions and have the efficiency to carry lightweight collars while in flight. The project will also require signal towers to communicate with each other and have the capability of measuring distance and scan landscape. The signal towers must not be excessively heavy or tall as they need to be manual carried to site area. A tower capable of being telescopic would be ideal.

Skills Required

The skills which would be required to make this project a reality would be software programming skills. After researching the programming of drones, I feel python language would be a key programming language needed to program the drone. Cloud computing skills would be necessary to create deck drone software so users can upload building plans for syncing with the perimeter identified by the signal towers.
